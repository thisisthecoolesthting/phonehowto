## How to Use eSIM on iPhone and Transfer Between Phones

Embedded SIM (eSIM) technology is becoming increasingly popular, especially in modern iPhones. Unlike traditional physical SIM cards, an eSIM is a digital SIM that allows you to activate a cellular plan without having to insert a physical card. This flexibility is especially useful for using multiple phone numbers on one device, managing work and personal lines, or traveling internationally. This guide covers how to set up and use an eSIM on your iPhone, and the process of transferring eSIMs between devices.

---

## What Is eSIM?

An eSIM is a programmable SIM chip embedded inside your phone’s hardware. It functions like a regular SIM card but eliminates the need to physically swap cards. Instead, you download your carrier’s profile directly onto your device. Many cellular providers support eSIM activation, allowing you to switch plans or add secondary lines without visiting a store or waiting for a physical SIM.

---

## How to Use eSIM on iPhone

Recent iPhones (iPhone XS, XR, 11 series, 12 series, and later) support eSIM functionality. Here’s how to set up and activate an eSIM on your iPhone:

### 1. Ensure Compatibility and Carrier Support

- Confirm that your carrier supports eSIM activation.
- Make sure your iPhone is updated to the latest iOS version for optimal eSIM functionality.

### 2. Obtain an eSIM QR Code or Activation Details

Your carrier will provide an eSIM activation method, often through a QR code, activation code, or via their app. This might be emailed to you or accessed through your carrier’s website or customer portal.

### 3. Set Up eSIM

- Open the **Settings** app on your iPhone.
- Tap **Cellular** or **Mobile Data**.
- Tap **Add Cellular Plan**.
- Use the iPhone camera to scan the QR code provided by your carrier, or enter the activation details manually if needed.
- Follow the on-screen instructions to complete the activation.

### 4. Label and Customize Your Plans

If you have multiple plans (a physical SIM plus eSIM, or multiple eSIMs), you can label each line (e.g., “Primary,” “Work,” “Travel”) to help you identify them.

### 5. Choose Default Lines and Settings

- Set which number is used for default voice calls, messages, and cellular data.
- You can enable or disable specific lines according to your needs.

## Using Dual SIMs on iPhone

Most iPhones with eSIM support also allow dual SIM functionality:

- One physical SIM card + one eSIM.
- Two eSIM profiles (on models that support multiple eSIMs).

This means you can have separate numbers for personal and business use or keep an international data plan alongside your home carrier.

## Transferring eSIM Between iPhones

When upgrading to a new iPhone, transferring your eSIM plan is generally straightforward but depends on your carrier’s policies and the iOS version.

### Method 1: Transfer Directly During Setup (iOS 16 and Later)

Apple introduced a feature in iOS 16 allowing direct transfer of eSIM profiles from your old iPhone during the setup process.

**Steps:**

- Turn on your new iPhone and start the setup.
- When prompted, approach it near your current iPhone, which should show a prompt to transfer your cellular plan.
- Follow on-screen instructions to move the eSIM to the new device.
- Confirm the transfer on both iPhones.
  
This method avoids contacting your carrier and does not require scanning QR codes again.

### Method 2: Use Carrier App or Contact Carrier Support

If your carrier doesn’t support direct transfer or you experience issues:

- Use your carrier’s app to move the eSIM to your new device, if available.
- Alternatively, contact the carrier to deactivate the eSIM on the old device and activate it on the new one.
- You may be given a new QR code or activation code to scan.

### Method 3: Use a Physical SIM if Available

If you have a physical SIM card, you can remove it from the old phone and insert it into the new one, then add the eSIM separately by scanning a QR code or activating a plan.

## Tips for Managing eSIM on iPhone

- **Backup Your Data**: Before transferring or resetting, back up your iPhone via iCloud or computer. Note that eSIM profiles are not usually included in backups for security reasons.
- **Keep Carrier Details Handy**: Have your carrier’s customer support information ready in case you need assistance reactivating an eSIM.
- **Manage Multiple eSIMs**: You can store multiple eSIM plans on your iPhone but activate only one at a time to switch lines.
- **Traveling with eSIM**: Consider purchasing a local eSIM data plan in your destination country without swapping physical SIM cards.
- **Restoring After Reset**: If you erase your iPhone, you may need to reactivate your eSIM via QR code or carrier app unless you use direct transfer methods.

---

## Troubleshooting eSIM Issues

- **eSIM Not Activating**: Check carrier support, iOS version, and try reconnecting to Wi-Fi during activation.
- **No Cellular Service**: Toggle airplane mode, restart your iPhone, or remove and re-add the eSIM plan.
- **Unable to Transfer eSIM**: Use the carrier’s app or contact support for reactivation assistance.

By understanding how to manage eSIMs effectively, you can enjoy multiple lines, easier travel communications, and seamless phone upgrades.
