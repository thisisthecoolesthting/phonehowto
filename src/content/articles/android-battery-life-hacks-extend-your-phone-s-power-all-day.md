## Optimize Your Screen Settings  

Your display is the biggest battery drain. Start by reducing brightness to 50% or lower—most screens remain perfectly readable at this level. Enable **adaptive brightness** (found in *Settings > Display*) to let your phone adjust automatically based on ambient light.  

Switch to **dark mode** wherever possible. OLED screens (common in flagship Androids) power off black pixels, saving energy. Some apps like Twitter and YouTube offer system-wide dark themes.  

Lower your **screen timeout** to 30 seconds or less (*Settings > Display > Screen timeout*). If you use an always-on display (AOD), disable it or set it to tap-to-show to minimize background power use.  

## Tame Background Apps and Processes  

Android’s **Adaptive Battery** (in *Settings > Battery*) learns your app usage and restricts background activity for rarely used apps. Enable it if your phone supports it.  

Manually restrict background activity for power-hungry apps:  
1. Go to *Settings > Apps*  
2. Select an app (e.g., Facebook, Instagram)  
3. Tap *Battery > Background restriction*  

Disable **background data** for non-essential apps (*Settings > Network & internet > Data usage > App data usage*).  

## Fine-Tune Connectivity Features  

**Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and GPS** drain power when left on. Turn them off when not in use, or use **Quick Settings** toggles for easy access.  

- **Wi-Fi:** Disable "Scanning always available" (*Settings > Location > Wi-Fi scanning*).  
- **Bluetooth:** Turn off if you’re not using headphones or a smartwatch.  
- **GPS:** Switch to **Battery Saver** mode (*Settings > Location > Mode*) for apps like Google Maps.  

**Mobile data** consumes more power than Wi-Fi. If you’re in a weak signal area, enable **Airplane Mode** temporarily to stop your phone from constantly searching for a signal.  

## Manage Notifications and Sync  

Reduce **push notifications** for apps that constantly wake your screen. Review apps in *Settings > Notifications* and disable non-essential alerts.  

Limit **auto-sync** for email and cloud services. Switch to manual sync or increase intervals (*Settings > Accounts > Auto-sync data*).  

## Use Battery Saver Mode Wisely  

Android’s built-in **Battery Saver** (under *Settings > Battery*) throttles performance, restricts background data, and disables visual effects. Enable it at 20% or set a custom trigger (e.g., 30%).  

For more control, try **Extreme Battery Saver** (on Pixel and some Samsung devices), which pauses all but essential apps until you disable it.  

## Optimize Performance Settings  

High refresh rates (90Hz/120Hz) drain batteries faster. If your phone supports it, switch to **60Hz** in *Settings > Display > Refresh rate*.  

Disable **animations** to reduce GPU load:  
1. Enable **Developer Options** (tap *Build number* in *About phone* 7 times).  
2. Navigate to *Developer Options > Drawing* and set **Window**, **Transition**, and **Animator scales** to "0.5x" or "Off."  

## Choose the Right Accessories  

A **high-quality charger** matters. Look for **USB-C PD (Power Delivery)** chargers for efficient fast charging without overheating. Avoid cheap, uncertified chargers—they can degrade battery health over time.  

**Power banks** are lifesavers for heavy users. Opt for ones with **18W+ PD support** and at least **10,000mAh** capacity.  

For wireless charging, pick a **Qi-certified charger** with **cooling fans** to prevent overheating during prolonged use.  

## What to Look for in Battery-Friendly Accessories  

- **Chargers:** USB-C PD 3.0, 18W+ output, compact design.  
- **Power banks:** 10,000–20,000mAh, PD support, lightweight.  
- **Cables:** Braided nylon, MFi-certified (if using USB-C to Lightning).  
- **Wireless chargers:** Qi-certified, 10W+ output, built-in cooling.  

## Monitor and Maintain Battery Health  

Check **battery usage stats** (*Settings > Battery > Battery usage*) weekly to spot power-hungry apps.  

Avoid **extreme temperatures**—heat degrades lithium-ion batteries fastest. Never leave your phone in a hot car or direct sunlight while charging.  

Charge between **20%–80%** for long-term health. Use **accidental charge limit** apps (like *Battery Charge Limit*) if your phone doesn’t natively support it.  

Replace your battery if capacity drops below 80%—most Android batteries last **2–3 years** with heavy use.  

By combining these tweaks with mindful charging habits, you’ll squeeze hours of extra life from your Android daily.
